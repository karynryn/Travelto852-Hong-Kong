Bus

Hong Kong’s bus system is extensive and reaches areas that the MTR and tram do not, including beaches, rural villages, mountainous regions, and hiking trails. The buses are modern, comfortable, and increasingly accessible, with many featuring low floors, wheelchair ramps, and priority seating. Bus routes and schedules are available on apps and maps, making it easier to plan your journey.

The bus system offers great scenic routes, especially those heading to outlying areas like Stanley, Sai Kung, and Shek O. It’s a convenient way to experience a different side of Hong Kong. However, buses can be delayed by traffic, and for first-time visitors, understanding the route numbers and stops can be confusing. Travel times can also be longer compared to the MTR. Bus fares vary by distance and route, typically ranging from HKD 4 to 50. Airport routes, such as the A21, cost more (about HKD 33). Payments can be made via Octopus Card or exact cash (no change is given).
