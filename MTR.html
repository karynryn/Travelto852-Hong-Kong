Mass Transit Railway

The MTR is Hong Kong’s primary mode of public transport and one of the most efficient subway systems in the world. It connects nearly every major district, including Hong Kong Island, Kowloon, the New Territories, Lantau Island, and even the airport and Disneyland. The system is extremely accessible, with most stations equipped with elevators, escalators, tactile paving for the visually impaired, and signage in multiple languages (English, Cantonese, and Mandarin). It's also integrated with many shopping malls and business centers, making it easy to get around the city. The trains are fast, reliable, air-conditioned, and punctual, making them a favorite for both locals and tourists.

However, the MTR can be very crowded during peak hours, and some older stations may not have full accessibility features such as elevators. Additionally, while it covers most urban and popular areas, it does not reach certain rural or remote hiking destinations. Fares start from about HKD 4 and increase with distance, with the Airport Express costing more (around HKD 115 from the airport to Central). Using an Octopus Card can offer small discounts and make the ride more convenient.
